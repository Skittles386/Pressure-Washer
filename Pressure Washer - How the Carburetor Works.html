In the bottom of the carburetor, there is a small plastic piece called a float that acts as a vacuum by pulling
in fuel from the fuel tank. The fuel is pushed through a small hole called a jet. The jet is mixed with the air and goes
to the piston which is ignited by a spark plug that will leave through the exhaust.

The engine can run rich, which means that there is more fuel than air. This can be bad if the engine runs too long because it can
flood the piston chamber. The only time the engine needs to run is when it is cold outside when you are trying to start it.
If the engine is running, lean (running lean is when there is too little fuel or less fuel mixed with air) it so there
is not enough fuel in the piston chamber. If this happens for a long time, it will crack the engine
block and destroy every part of the motor. ______________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________






1. Choke Valve - Allows air to flow through the carburetor


2. Venturi - The narrow passage increases the velocity of the air stream and hence reduce the pressure of the air


3. Trottle Valve - Controls the flow of air and thus the quantity of the air/fuel mixture delivered to the engine


4. Float Chamber - Automatically regulates the supply of fuel, leveling the float to a appropriate amount of fuel
